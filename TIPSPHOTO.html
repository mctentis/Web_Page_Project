Composition Tips

  • Use the rule of thirds for balanced shots
  • Look for leading lines to guide the viewer's eye
  • Experiment with different perspectives and angles
  • Pay attention to negative space
  • Use natural frames within your environment

Lighting Techniques

  • Shoot during golden hour for warm, soft light
  • Use reflectors to bounce and diffuse harsh light
  • Learn to read and use histogram displays
  • Experiment with backlighting for dramatic effects
  • Understand the direction and quality of light

Equipment Advice

  • Always carry extra batteries and memory cards
  • Use a tripod for long exposures and low light
  • Invest in quality lenses over camera bodies
  • Clean your gear regularly
  • Learn your camera's manual settings

Editing Best Practices

  • Shoot in RAW format for maximum editing flexibility
  • Maintain natural skin tones in portrait editing
  • Use masking for localized adjustments
  • Keep backups of original files
  • Develop a consistent editing style
